Types of Running Races in Modern Day Athletics

Sprints

100m, 200m, 400m

Sprinting is running over a short distance in a limited period of time. It is used in many sports that incorporate running, typically as a way of quickly reaching a target or goal, or avoiding or catching an opponent.

Middle Distance

800m, 1500m

Middle-distance running events are track races longer than sprints, up to 3000 metres. The standard middle distances are the 800 metres and 1500 metres.

Long Distance

At least 3000m

Long-distance running, or endurance running, is a form of continuous running over distances of at least 3 kilometres (1.8 miles). Physiologically, it is largely aerobic in nature and requires stamina as well as mental strength.

Hurdles

110/100m, 400m

Hurdling is the act of running and jumping over an obstacle at speed.[1] In the early 19th century, hurdlers ran at and jumped over each hurdle, landing on both feet and checking their forward motion.

Relays

4x100m, 4x400m

A relay race is a racing competition where members of a team take turns completing parts of racecourse or performing a certain action. Relay races take the form of professional races and amateur games.
